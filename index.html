About me

Hi, I'm Connor! I'm a second year master's student studying statistics at BYU, advised by Dr. Nate Sandholtz.

My research has analyzed sports problems through a statistical lens, applying novel inverse optimization methods to understand decision making. For example, we have explored fourth down decision making in the NFL by assuming that coaches have beliefs about how likely different outcomes are, then estimating those believed probabilities. Through this lens, we have been able to explain and understand coach decision making in a way no research has ever done before.

I'm currently applying to Ph.D. programs in statistics!
